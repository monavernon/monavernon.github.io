Longer

Neha Narula is the Director of the Digital Currency Initiative, a part of the MIT Media Lab focusing on cryptocurrencies and blockchain technology. She received her PhD in computer science from MIT in 2015, where she worked on concurrency control for scalable distributed systems and databases. Her current research interests are in cryptocurrencies and distributed systems. She has designed and taught three courses at MIT on cryptocurrencies and blockchains, and is a lecturer at Sloan.

Neha was a member of the World Economic Forum's Global Futures Council on Blockchain 2016-2017 and has given a TED talk on the future of money. She was named to Fortune's Ledger 40 under 40 list in 2018. In a previous life, she helped relaunch the news aggregator Digg and from 2003-2009 was a software engineer at Google. There, she was on the team that initially designed Blobstore, a system for storing and serving petabytes of immutable data, and she worked on Froogle, a shopping website, and Native Client, a way to run native code securely through a browser. Her work has appeared in the New York Times, PBS NewsHour, CNBC, TechCrunch, and Wired.
